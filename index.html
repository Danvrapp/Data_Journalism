Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income, & poverty level

Health risks vs. poverty

When weighing poverty vs. the health risks measured, there appears to be a consistent, although slight, positive coorelation. I would hypothesize this goes along with the idea that a lower income is tied to a lower education level, therefore not knowing and understanding the risks involed in being obese & smoking. Lower income families tend to eat a less nutrious diet, and eat a diet higher in carbs, fat, & sugar.

Health risks vs. age

When weighing age vs. the health risks measured, I see no obvious, apparent trends. This does not surprise me, as age groups encompass all walks of life and all socio-economic backgrounds.

Health risks vs. income

When weighing income vs. the health risks measured, there appears to be a negative coorelation with smoking and obesity. Again, this does not surprise me, and I would hypothesize this is because higher income most likely follows higher education levels, where individuals would know and have a better understanding of the negative effects of smoking and obesity. What I do find surprising is there appears to be no coorelation--and definitely no positive coorelation--between income and percentage of invividuals having healthcare.
